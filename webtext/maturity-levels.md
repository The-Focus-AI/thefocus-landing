## Focus.AI — AI Capability Maturity Framework

### Core Thesis

AI adoption isn't about cost savings or headcount reduction. It's an **innovation enabler** that unlocks capabilities organizations literally couldn't access before. The journey is from **tribal to coherent** — moving information, knowledge, and processes from ad hoc and siloed to structured, integrated, and continuously evolving.

---

### Dimension 1: Maturity Levels (The Backbone)

**Level 0 — None.** No AI adoption. Everything is manual. Knowledge lives in people's heads or scattered documents. Tribal knowledge dominates.

**Level 1 — Ad Hoc.** Employees are individually using ChatGPT, Copilot, etc. Copy-paste workflows. No organizational structure, no data integration, no governance. Inconsistent and unscalable.

**Level 2 — Integrated Tooling.** Organization has adopted vendor tools or built integrations into existing workflows. AI is connected to systems like GitHub, CRMs, analytics platforms. There's some structure and consistency.

**Level 3 — Custom Data Intelligence.** AI is connected to proprietary internal datasets. Teams can ask ad hoc questions and get instant answers (the 45-minutes-to-5-seconds transformation). Exploratory analysis becomes possible. This is where real capability expansion begins.

**Level 4 — Dynamic and Reactive.** Systems pull from production data, customer behavior, service logs. AI proactively surfaces insights and adapts. Organizational roles shift — product managers and customer-facing teams start driving what used to be engineering work. Power moves to the people closest to the business problem.

**Level 5 — Autonomous and Evolving.** Agent sessions are mined for institutional knowledge. Tribal knowledge crystallizes into documented workflows and specs. Feedback loops are continuous. The system learns and improves on its own. Competitive advantage compounds.

---

### Dimension 2: Function Types

These cut across every department and mature at different rates.

**Knowledge Retrieval** — Finding answers. Goes from manual lookup to instant conversational access to proprietary data.

**Content Generation** — Creating stuff. Goes from templates and manual drafting to AI-generated content with brand consistency and compliance baked in.

**Decision Support** — Making choices. Goes from gut instinct and slow analysis to real-time scenario modeling and recommendations.

**Process Automation** — Doing repetitive tasks. Goes from manual execution to fully automated workflows that self-correct.

**Predictive Intelligence** — Anticipating what's next. Goes from historical reporting to proactive pattern detection and forecasting.

---

### Dimension 3: Risk & Governance

This is the dimension that gets you in the door with enterprise buyers. Different function types carry different risk profiles, and governance needs to match.

**Level 0 — No Oversight.** People are using AI tools with zero organizational awareness. Shadow AI. Nobody knows what data is going where.

**Level 1 — Basic Usage Policies.** Organization has published acceptable use guidelines. Employees know what tools are sanctioned. Basic "don't put customer data in ChatGPT" rules.

**Level 2 — Compliance Frameworks.** AI use is mapped to existing regulatory requirements (GDPR, SOC 2, HIPAA, industry-specific). Data handling policies are in place. Vendor assessments are happening.

**Level 3 — Audit Trails and Monitoring.** Every AI interaction is logged and traceable. There's visibility into what data goes in, what comes out, and who accessed what. Model outputs can be reviewed and challenged.

**Level 4 — Governed Autonomous Systems.** AI systems operate with defined boundaries and escalation paths. Human-in-the-loop at critical decision points. Continuous monitoring for drift, bias, and accuracy. Regular review cycles.

**Level 5 — Adaptive Governance.** Governance itself evolves as AI adoption deepens. Policies update dynamically based on new use cases and risks. Organization has dedicated AI governance roles or teams. Frameworks are proactive, not reactive.

**Key insight for selling:** Different functions need different governance levels. Marketing experimenting with content generation can operate at Level 1 governance. But AI touching HR decisions, financial data, or customer-facing products needs Level 3-4 before you even start. This creates a natural roadmap — move fast where risk is low, build guardrails where it's high.

---

### Dimension 4: Department Mapping

**Software Development**
- L1: Devs using ChatGPT for code lookups and answers
- L2: AI integrated into dev workflow — code suggestions, bug catching, design assistance
- L3: Spec-driven development — product managers write requirements, AI builds features
- L4: Production data and performance metrics feed back into priorities automatically
- L5: Agent sessions mined for specs and workflows; PMs and customer service reps effectively pushing code; engineering becomes enablement, not a bottleneck

**Data Analytics / Marketing**
- L1: Analysts manually building reports over days or weeks
- L2: AI tools helping generate visualizations and summaries faster
- L3: Instant ad hoc queries — anyone can ask a question, get an answer in 5 seconds
- L4: Exploratory analysis — discovering patterns nobody thought to ask about
- L5: Continuous intelligence feeding directly into strategy and decision-making

**HR / Hiring**
- L1: Job descriptions written manually, screening done by hand
- L2: AI generating job descriptions, automating initial screening
- L3: Meetings tracked, institutional knowledge documented rather than tribal
- L4: Predictive analytics on culture fit, retention, workforce planning
- L5: Hiring and staffing dynamically adapts based on organizational needs and performance data

**Not yet detailed:** Sales, Customer Success, Finance/Accounting, System Integration

---

### Dimension 5: Who's Driving Adoption

- Individual contributors experimenting on their own
- Team-level adoption with some coordination
- Department-wide mandates with leadership buy-in
- Cross-functional integration breaking down silos
- Company-wide strategy with AI embedded in how the business operates

---

### Dimension 6: Build vs. Buy

- Using off-the-shelf tools (ChatGPT, vendor platforms)
- Customizing vendor platforms for internal needs
- Building internal tools on top of AI APIs
- Building proprietary systems connected to internal data
- Building systems that learn, evolve, and compound advantage

**"Fix your vibe coded app"** sits right at the Level 1-to-2 bridge here. Someone built something with ad hoc AI, hit the wall, and needs engineering discipline to make it production-ready. Natural entry point that upsells into the full maturity framework.

---

### The Sales Narrative

Walk into a company and show them the matrix. They can self-identify where they are on each dimension. The conversation becomes: "You're at Level 1 overall, Level 2 in engineering, Level 0 in governance. Here's what the path forward looks like, and here's where we'd start." It's non-threatening because it meets them where they are, and it creates a clear roadmap with natural expansion points. Every level up is a new engagement.
